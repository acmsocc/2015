Call for posters

The SoCC poster sessions provide an opportunity to present works-in-progress in an informal setting. Topics of interest are the same as the research topics in the SoCC conference call for papers. While anyone can submit a proposal, preference will be given to posters where the primary contribution is from one or more students, or from an industry/start-up organization. Students must present student posters at the conference.

Poster submission

Authors and students are invited to submit posters to communicate the essence of a research problem - why the problem matters, techniques used to solve the problem, any initial results, and the novelty and importance.

Submission guidelines

We encourage the posters to follow the guidelines for consideration.

  • For the poster to be effective, we encourage you to read the article Creating an effective poster by John Wilkes.

  • Include a URL providing additional information about your work to the attendees.

  • Indicate if the submission should be considered for the student or industry/start-up best-poster award.

  • Accepted posters should be A0 paper size (841 x 1189mm); portrait mode is recommended.

  • By submitting a poster, you grant SoCC permission to post the poster on this web site, if accepted. (You'll be given an opportunity to modify before posting)

Review process

Submissions are single blind, so please include authors' names and affiliation.
